Rule 1 Definitions

Terms used in these conditions of carriage shall be defined as follows;

1. “Jeju Air” means Jeju Air, Incorporated.
2. “Passenger” means any person, except members of the crew, carried or to be carried in an aircraft with the consent of Carrier.
3. “Carriage” which is equivalent to transportation, means carriage of passenger and/or baggage by air, gratuitously or for reward.
4. “Carrier” means an air carrier that also includes the carrier that can issues a ticket and is obliged to carry passengers or their baggage under this ticket or to perform related other services for air carrier.
5. “Flight tickets” are passenger and baggage claim or electronic tickets (e-tickets) issued by the carrier and include all terms and conditions, coupons that are part of the ticket.
A. Composition of passenger ticket
1) Flight Coupon: The coupon is a part of a flight ticket that specifies a specific section to be transported by the passenger, and is exchanged for a boarding pass when you check-in.
2) Passenger coupon / passenger receipt: This is a part of flight ticket which can prove the contract of carriage with the passenger.
 6. “Conjunction Ticket” means two or more tickets concurrently issued to a passenger and which constitute a single contract of carriage.
7. "All freight charges and Fee” means the air fare, fuel surcharge, airport usage fee, and all charges set by Jeju Air for air transportation.
8. "Baggage” means the passenger's goods, supplies and other personal items which is prepared from the passenger for comfort or convenience, and includes all checked and carry-on baggage unless otherwise specified.
9. “Checked Baggage” means a piece of baggage, surrendered by a passenger with a valid ticket, that the Carrier has taken custody of and has issued a baggage check tag for.
10. “Unchecked Baggage” means baggage carried and taken by a passenger and kept in cabin.
11. “Excess Baggage” means baggage in excess of the free baggage allowance permitted by the Carrier.
12. “Baggage Tag” means the portion of the ticket issued by the Carrier to confirm the carriage of a passenger’s checked baggage. It consists of a baggage identification tag attached to the checked baggage and a baggage claim tag handed over to the passenger.
13. “Adult” means a passenger who is 12 years of age or over.
14. “Child” means a passenger who is 2 years of age or over but under 12 years of age.
15. “Unaccompanied minor” means a passenger over the age of 5 years who is not accompanied together with their guardian or an adult passenger when they take a plane.
16. “Infant” means a passenger who is under 2 years of age.
17. “Check-in deadline” means the time defined by Jeju Air for a passenger to complete Check-in and receipt of Boarding Pass.
18. “Tariff” means the fare, rates and charges applicable to the carriage of passengers and baggage by Jeju Air and also includes the rules and procedures related to the carriage, which form part of carriage clause.
19. “Destination” means the ultimate stopping place according to the contract of carriage.
20. “Stop-over”, which is a synonym of the stop of travel, means that a passenger stops the trip intentionally in the point between starting point and destination under Carrier’s prior consent.
21. “United stated of America or The United States or The USA” means, unless otherwise specified, the area comprising the 51 contiguous federated states, the Federal District of Columbia, Alaska, Hawaii, Guam, Puerto Rico, St. Croix and St. Thomas of the Virgin Islands, American Samoa, The Canal Zone, Canton, Midway and Wake islands.
22. “Round trip”, which is equivalent to a return journey, means travel from one point to another and return by the same air route used outbound whether or not the fares outbound and inbound be the same, or travel from one point to another and return by an air route different from that used outbound, for which the same normal through, one-way fare is established.
23. “Damage” includes death, injury, delay, loss or other damage of whatsoever nature arising out of or in connection with carriage or other services performed by carrier incidental thereto.
24. “Convention” means the Convention for the Unification of Certain Rules Relating to International Carriage by Air, signed at Warsaw, October 12, 1929, (hereinafter called “the Warsaw Convention”) or that Convention as amended at the Hague, September 28, 1955 (hereinafter called “the Warsaw Convention as amended at the Hague, 1955”), or “the Convention for the Unification of Certain Rules Relating to International Carriage by Air” amended at Montreal May 28, 1999, (hereinafter called “the Montreal Convention”), whichever may be applicable.
25. “International carriage other than that defined by the convention” means any carriage which is not international carriage as defined by the Warsaw Convention or the Warsaw Convention as amended at The Hague, 1955, or the Montreal Convention as amended at Montreal, 1999, but in which, according to the agreement made by the parties, the place of departure and the place of landing are situated in more than one country. As used therein, the term “country” which is equivalent to “state” includes all territory subject to its sovereignty, suzerainty, mandate, trusteeship, or authority.
26. “SDR (Special Drawing Right)” means the Special Drawing Rights defined by the International Monetary Fund. (Hereinafter called “IMF”).
27. “French gold francs” means French francs consisting of 65 1/2 milligrams of gold at the standard of fineness of nine hundred thousandths.
28. “Days” means calendar days, including Sundays and legal holidays; provided that for the purpose of notification, the day upon which the notice is dispatched shall not be counted; and that for purpose of determining duration of validity, the balance the day upon which the ticket is issued, or flight commenced shall not be counted.

Rule 2 Standard Format of Electronic Rules

1. Application and Other Conditions (Category 50)
This category is used to specify the rule title, geographic application, capacity limitations, rules not applicable, journey types/types of transportation, and other conditions. This category has no impact on itinerary pricing. When this category is absent, a rule application and other conditions paragraph do not exist. There is no impact on itinerary pricing.
2. Eligibility (Category 1)
This category defines the identification requirement and age range for a particular passenger type. When this category is absent or not applicable to the passenger involved, then no specific age or identification requirements exist.
3. Day/Time (Category 2)
This category defines times and/or days when travel is permitted or not permitted. The day/time provision applies to the origin of each fare component scheduled to depart during that time/day period, unless stated otherwise. When this category is absent or not applicable, the fare can be used at any time on any day of the week.
4. Seasonality (Category 3)
This category defines the seasonal dates for a fare. It is assumed that the seasonal date applies from the origin portion of the fare (origin of the priceable unit), implying that the seasonal level in effect for the origin portion of the fare is used for all subsequent travel regardless of date, unless stated otherwise. When this category is absent or not applicable, there are no seasonal date restrictions for the fare.
5. Flight Application (Category 4)
This category reflects information that is basically flight number– or flight pattern–oriented. It may be used to reflect either positive or negative application of this information. This category will not reflect routing tariff provisions. When this category is absent or not applicable, there are no flight number or flight pattern restrictions besides those specified in the routing.
6. Advance Reservations/ Ticketing (Category 5)
This category is used to define reservations and ticketing requirements applicable to a fare. It is assumed that the reservation and ticketing requirements apply from the origin portion of the fare (origin of the priceable unit). This category also indicates which sectors of travel, if any, must be confirmed before ticketing. The advance reservation and ticketing conditions shown in this category apply to the original ticket issuance. It will be assumed that standbys and waitlisting are permitted for any flight or any date unless information, contained in the record to the contrary. When this category is absent, the fare has no advance reservation or ticketing requirements and that open segments, including open returns are permitted.
7. Minimum Stay (Category 6)
This category specifies the earliest that return travel may commence. The day of departure is not counted in calculating elapsed time for minimum stay. When this category is absent or not applicable, there are no minimum stay requirements.
8. Maximum Stay (Category 7)
This category indicates the latest that return travel may commence or may be completed. The day of departure should not be counted in calculating elapsed time for maximum stay. When this category is absent or not applicable, no maximum stay requirements, except ticket validity, apply to the fare.
9. Stopovers (Category 8) This category defines the conditions under which stopovers are permitted and the applicable charges. It is assumed that stopovers are not permitted unless this category is present. However, when stopovers are allowed, it is assumed that they may be made at any point along the carrier’s route. When this category is absent or not applicable, it is assumed that stopovers are not permitted.
10. Transfers (Category 9)
This category defines the conditions under which transfers may occur and the applicable charges. When this category is absent or not applicable, unlimited transfers are permitted within the bounds of the routing and no restrictions apply to these transfers.
11. Combinations (Category 10)
This category supports combinations that are permitted with specified fares and other fares and add-ons to create one-way, round-trip, circle-trip and open-jaw transportation. Combinability is the process of using multiple fares or half of round-trip fares to arrive at a complete fare to be charged to the passenger. The following assumptions will apply unless otherwise specified:
A. Open Jaws/Round Trips/Circle Trips and End-on-End combinations are not permitted.
B. International fares may be combined with add-ons unless specifically prohibited/restricted.
C. When Open Jaws/Round Trips/Circle Trips are permitted, unless otherwise stated, the following assumptions apply:
1) Combinations are permitted with any carrier.
2) Combinations are permitted with any tariff and any rule.
3) Combinations are permitted with any round trip or one-way fare.
4) For Open-Jaws, the open segment can be between any two geographic points.
 12. Blackout Dates (Category 11)
This Category defines single dates or date ranges when travel is not permitted. The assumption is that blackouts apply to the scheduled departure time of a flight regardless of the portion of the passenger’s travel they represent. When this category is absent, the fare is not subject to blackout dates.
13. Surcharges (Category 12)
This category defines the conditions under which surcharges are applicable and the corresponding charge. Surcharges are applied on a fare component basis, unless otherwise stated. When this category is absent or not applicable, no surcharge applies.
14. Accompanied Travel (Category 13)
This category is used to define when travel with one or more passengers is necessary to qualify for the fare. When this category is absent or not applicable, the passenger may travel alone over the entire routing.
15. Travel Restrictions (Category 14)
This category is used to state specific travel date restrictions. These include the dates when the fare may first be used, travel commencement, travel expiration, or travel completion. When this category is absent or not applicable, there are no specific travel date restrictions within the rule.
16. Sales Restrictions (Category 15)
This category is used when the sale of the fares is restricted by various conditions. These conditions include reservation and/or ticketing dates, countries and/or currencies of sale, form of payment, method of ticketing, who may sell the fare, and where the sale can be made.
A. Public Tariffs
When there is no Category 15 information, then the fare is available for sale, ticketing, and display at all times, anywhere, and by anyone within the confines of the other category provisions.
Private Tariffs
B. When there is no Category 15 information, then the fare is not available for sale, ticketing, and display at anytime, anywhere, and by anyone.
 17. Penalties (Category 16)
This category is used to define applicable penalties and charges. It is also used to specify the conditions under which re-routings may take place or tickets may be reissued. When this category is absent or not applicable, there are no penalties for a fare.
18. Hip/Mileage Exceptions (Category 17)
This category is used to negate the assumption or to specify that the Higher Intermediate Point rule applies only for stopovers and connection points. When this category is absent or not applicable, the Higher Intermediate Point Rule applies.
19. Ticket Endorsements (Category 18)
This category is used to indicate ticket endorsement text and the location on the ticket for the endorsement, either the Endorsement box or Form of Payment box. This category can also contain information on whether the endorsement is for the original ticket only, reissued tickets, or both. When this category is absent or not applicable, there are no ticket endorsement requirements for the fare.
20. Children Discounts (Category 19)
This category provides specific fare amounts or the method for calculating discount fares for children and infants. It also specifies accompanying travel requirements. Unless otherwise stated, it is assumed that children/infants must be accompanied on all sectors in the same compartment by an adult at least 12 years of age. When this category is absent or not applicable, the fare is not discountable for children or infants.
21. Tour Conductor Discounts (Category 20)
This category provides the specific fare amounts or the information for calculating discount fares for tour conductors. It also specifies accompanying travel requirements. When this category is absent or not applicable, the fare is not discountable for tour conductors.
